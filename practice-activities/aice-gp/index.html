01

What This Artifact Is

Unit 1 — Foundations

11 weeks. Sit time scales from 25 minutes (Week 1) to 75 minutes (Week 11) in 5-minute steps. Passages grow from ~200 to ~500 words. Essay targets scale from ~200 to 600–900 words.

Topics rotate across all three Cambridge GP domains: Economic & Political, Science & Environment, and Literature & Media.

Unit 2 — Advanced

Same 11-week time scaling as Unit 1. Difficulty starts at Unit 1 Week 6 level and ramps exponentially. Passages are more complex, vocabulary is richer, questions demand more nuanced argumentation.

Completing both units gives you 22 timed Paper 1 practice sittings before the June 2027 examination.

04

The Homework Loop — Four Tasks

Every session generates four homework tasks. They can be done in any order, but the loop is designed to flow from the essay outward — from correction to vocabulary to rehearsal. Every minute you invest earns XP. There is no deadline on any individual task except the one you set yourself.

Task 1 — Vocab Mining: Reading Passage VJ XP

  1. In class, before you leave: re-read the passage and highlight every word or phrase you could not have written yourself. Enter each into your Personalised Vocab Journal (PVJ) with your best guess at the meaning. This is batching.
  2. Homework: research each word properly — definition, example sentence, word family. This is your Research Batch.
  3. When the batch is complete, notify Coach Stew. A /cq (comprehension quiz) will be set on the passage. Passing it greenlights the passage as a Rehearsal script. RS XP

Task 2 — Essay Redraft Loop MBD XP

  1. Read your /faaice assessment in full when Coach Stew delivers it. Start with the Socratic Questions. Do not begin rewriting until you have worked through them.
  2. Redraft your essay on your own time — 10 minutes to 2 hours. Every minute of redraft time earns MBD XP. Submit via the scorecard when ready.
  3. Coach Stew provides a second round of assessment. You may continue this loop as many times as you choose. Each round earns more MBD XP.
  4. When you decide you are done, tell Coach Stew. This releases the AI-generated rewrite of your most up-to-date draft. Proceed to Task 3.

Task 3 — Vocab Mining: AI Essay VJ XP

  1. Repeat Task 1 exactly — but using the AI-generated essay as your source text instead of the original passage.
  2. Batch new words into PVJ. Research the batch as your next homework task.
  3. Notify Coach Stew for a /cq on the AI essay script. Passing it greenlights it as a second Rehearsal script. RS XP

Task 4 — Rehearsal RS XP

  1. Once a script is green-lit, rehearse it to the standard agreed with Coach Stew.
  2. Log each completed rehearsal set on your scorecard.
  3. Both the original passage and the AI essay can be active scripts simultaneously — each earns its own RS XP.
05

XP Available Per Week

In-class XP

  • MBD XP — your in-class writing time (from the moment you click Begin Reading to the moment you submit)

Homework XP

  • MBD XP — every minute of essay redrafting (Task 2)
  • VJ XP — vocabulary batch entries and research (Tasks 1 & 3)
  • RS XP — rehearsal sets on green-lit scripts (Task 4)

The compounding effect

As weeks progress and you internalise the homework loop, less in-class time is needed for setup and explanation. This leaves more time for the ever-expanding weekly sit. By Week 11 of Unit 2 you are sitting a full 75-minute Cambridge Paper 1 cold — with a complete five-machine XP cycle running in parallel.

06

A Few Practical Notes

Changing weeks mid-session

If you navigate to a different week while a session is in progress, you will be asked to confirm. Your current essay will not be saved. Always download the PDF before navigating away.

If time expires

The essay area locks automatically. This is intentional. Submit and download the PDF immediately — do not close the tab first. You can still receive /faaice feedback on a partial essay.

Word count guidance

The word counter turns gold when you reach the week target. Cambridge does not impose a hard word limit on Paper 1, but examiners advise 600–900 words for a full-length response. Scaled targets in earlier weeks are calibrated to the time available.
